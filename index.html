ABOUT US

What's so special about Canberra?

Canberra is a youthful city with a significant architectural heritage. The city's architectural look is diverse but consciously unified, with significant phases of growth in the 1920s and from the 1950s to the 1980s.
The era of growth in Canberra from the mid-late twentieth century under the National Capital growth Commission's control (1958-1989) is not well-celebrated or acknowledged by the Canberra community.
Several buildings that are invaluable to Canberra have already been lost, and many more are at danger of being destroyed soon.

Who are Canberra Modern?

Canberra Modern is a dedicated group of history and design professionals that have a sincere affection for their city.
Edwina Jans is an archaeologist and cultural heritage specialist employed for GML Heritage. Edwina specialised on education, communication, and creative involvement with historic sites.
Amy Jarvis is a Senior Heritage Consultant with Philip Leeson Architects, specialising in heritage management and interpretation. Amy received a Churchill Fellowship in 2018/2019 to study advocacy and engagement approaches related to mid-century architecture in the USA.

Our Patron - Tim Ross

Tim 'Rosso' Ross, a TV and radio broadcaster, comedian, author, and self-proclaimed 'Design Nerd', has long been passionate about architecture and design. He has pushed for this field for many years, frequently using innovative and humorous approaches.
Tim, a dedicated supporter of Canberra Modern, has showcased numerous successful performances at the festival. He advocates for crucial matters in Australian architecture and design, delivering speeches across the country on conserving Australia's architectural heritage and honouring the legacy of the nation's prominent designers.
